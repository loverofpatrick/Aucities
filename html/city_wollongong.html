Wollongong

The Wollongong City Estimated Resident Population for 2021 is 214,657, with a population density of 313.9 persons per square km. Wollongong City is located in the Illawarra Region of New South Wales, about 80 kilometres south of the Sydney CBD.

Temperature

Warmest months are January and February, with an average high-temperature of 25.6°C (78.1°F).

With an average high-temperature of 17°C (62.6°F) and an average low-temperature of 8.3°C (46.9°F), July is the coldest month in Wollongong.

Rain

During the entire year, the rain falls for 137.3 days and collects up to 1320mm (51.97") of precipitation.

August is the least humid month, with an average relative humidity of 52%.The month with the least rainfall in Wollongong is July, when the rain falls for 8.5 days and typically collects 63mm (2.48") of precipitation.

With an average relative humidity of 69%, February is the most humid month.The month with the most rainfall in Wollongong, is March, when the rain falls for 14.5 days and typically aggregates up to 160mm (6.3") of precipitation.

Average Salary

The average salary in Wollongong, New South Wales is AU$25.69 per hour and AU$66,000 per year, according to Payscale statistics by November 2022.

The most popular occupations in Wollongong, New South Wales are Carpenter, Dental Assistant, and Medical Receptionist which pay between AU$19.53 and AU$38.45 per hour.

The most popular employers are NSW Health, Department of Education, and Stellar.

House Price

The median House sales price is AU$1,300,000, and the median house rent is $477-$725 per week for houses with 2-4 bedrooms, according to Realestate market survey in November 2022.

Unit and Apartment Price

The median Unit and Apartment sales price is $700,000, and the median rent is AU$460 per week for apartments with 2 bedrooms, according to Realestate market survey in November 2022

Ancestry

The most common ancestries in Wollongong were English 26.1%, Australian 24.5%, Irish 8.7%, Scottish 7.4% and Italian 4.3%.

Country of birth

72.3% of people were born in Australia. The most common countries of birth were England 3.7%, China (excludes SARs and Taiwan) 2.2%, Italy 1.2%, New Zealand 1.1% and India 0.9%.

Religion

The most common responses for religion were No Religion 27.2%, Catholic 26.1% and Anglican 17.9%.
