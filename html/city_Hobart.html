Hobart

Hobart is the capital and most populous city of the Australian island state of Tasmania.Home to almost half of all Tasmanians, it is the least-populated Australian state capital city, and second-smallest if territories are taken into account, before Darwin, Northern Territory.

Temperature

The warmest month is January, with an average high-temperature of 20.1°C (68.2°F) and an average low-temperature of 11.1°C (52°F).

With an average high-temperature of 10.9°C (51.6°F) and an average low-temperature of 5.1°C (41.2°F), July is the coldest month in Hobart.

With an average of 9.2h of sunshine, December has the most sunshine of the year in Hobart, Australia.The month with the least sunshine in Hobart is June, with an average of 5.3h of sunshine.

Rain

Throughout the year, in Canberra, there are 129.1 rainfall days, and 392mm (15.43") of precipitation is accumulated.

The most humid month in Hobart, Australia, is June, with an average relative humidity of 82%, whereas the rainiest months in Hobart, Australia, are August and November, with an average 38mm (1.5") of rainfall.

Average Salary

The average salary in Hobart, Tasmania is AU$25.52 per hour and AU$64,000 per year, according to Payscale statistics by November 2022.

The most popular occupations in Hobart, Tasmania are Accountant, Operations Manager, which pay between AU$48,000 and AU$108,000 per hour.

The most popular employers are Tassal, University of Tasmania, and Tasmanian Government.

House Price

The median House sales price is $754,640-$937,500 in capital city, and the median house rent is $575-$620 per week for 2-3 bedroom houses, according to Realestate market survey in November 2022.

Unit and Apartment Price

Ancestry

The most common ancestries in Hobart were English 29.4%, Australian 23.7%, Irish 9.7%, Scottish 8.3% and Chinese 4.4%.

Country of birth

In Hobart, 71.2% of people were born in Australia. The most common countries of birth were England 4.1%, China (excludes SARs and Taiwan) 3.5%, India 1.2%, New Zealand 1.1% and Malaysia 1.0%.

Religion

The most common responses for religion in Hobart were No Religion, so described 46.4%, Catholic 14.2%, Anglican 13.7%, Not stated 10.0% and Uniting Church 2.1%.
